**ITB-ICAO-00068**

**New VSAT-Radar Network**

**SET 1**

1)This is in reference to the Invitation to Bid Document, Section 2, Instruction to Bidders item #20. The ITB allows the formation of Joint Ventures, Consortiums, and Associations: How does ICAO differentiate between the 3 types of bidders?

**RESPONSE:**

ICAO does not distinguish between Joint Ventures, Consortiums, or Associations nor prefers any particular form of alliance. It is the responsibility of each bidder to determine the most appropriate structure.

Any group of legal entities, regardless of its form or legal constitution, shall be jointly and severally liable to ICAO for the fulfilment of the provisions of the Contract, if awarded.

Bidders participating as a Joint Venture, Consortium, or Association are reminded to complete **Form E** (document ITB-ICAO-00068 V2, page 26) clearly indicating the names of all partners, the proposed proportion of responsibilities (in percentage terms %), the type of goods and/or services to be performed by each partner, and the designation of a leading partner, if applicable.

2) This is in reference to the Invitation to Bid Document, Section 2, Instruction to Bidders item #27. This item indicates that the bidder shall inform ICAO of any change(s) of circumstances arising during the ITB process such as:

• a change affecting any declaration, accreditation, license or approval;

• major re-organizational changes, company re-structuring, a take-over, buy-out or similar event(s) affecting the operation and/or financing of the bidder or its major sub-contractors;

• a change to any information on which ICAO may rely in assessing bids.

If the bidder is a Consortium and one of the members of the Consortium is acquired by another company during the evaluation process, but the acquisition does not affect any declaration, accreditation, license or approval, and it doesn't affect the operation of the original consortium bidder because the original company continues to exist but under a larger and stronger Parent Company. We would like to ask the following:

a) Does this qualify as a major re-organizational change?

b) Does this disqualify the validity of the proposal?

c) Does it make any difference if the acquired company was or wasn't the lead entity?

**RESPONSE:**

In principle, an acquisition is considered a major re-organizational change. However, we do not dispose of sufficient information to assess the specific circumstances and provide a definitive response. The assessment will take into account the proposed allocation of responsibilities (expressed as percentages), the nature of the goods and/or services to be performed by the acquired partner within the consortium and how the acquisition could affect the ownership, control, management, and operations of that entity and ultimately the ability to deliver the goods or services in full compliance with the technical specifications.
